Artifact Evaluation for PPoPP 2017

[ Back to PPoPP 2017 conference website ]

Authors of accepted PPoPP 2017 papers will be invited to formally submit their supporting materials to the Artifact Evaluation process. The Artifact Evaluation process is run by a separate committee whose task is to assess how the artifacts support the work described in the papers. This submission is voluntary and will not influence the final decision regarding the papers. Papers that successfully go through the Artifact Evaluation process will receive a seal of approval printed on the papers themselves. Authors of accepted papers are then encouraged to make these materials publicly available upon publication of the proceedings, by including them as "source materials" in the Digital Library.

How to submit

Please, prepare your artifacts for submission using the following guide. Then, register your submission at the joint PPoPP/CGO EasyChair website (TBD) - you will be asked to submit your paper title, author list, artifact abstract, pdf of your paper with an appendix describing how to access and validate your artifacts, and possible conflicts of interests with AE members.

Reviewing process

Your artifacts will be reviewing based on the following guidelines. Artifacts receiving "met expectations" or above score will pass evaluation and will receive a stamp of approval. We are also arranging a prize for the highest ranked artifact.

Feedback

We consider Artifact Evaluation as a continuous learning curve - our eventual goal is to collaboratively develop common experimental and artifact sharing methodology for computer system's research.

Based on some encountered issues and your feedback, we also develop an open-source supporting technology particularly focusing on empirical experimentation, customization of experimental workflows (for example, to evaluate algorithm scalability, numerical stability, speed ups, etc), fair comparison of results (for example, for computer architecture modeling), and artifact reuse (Collective Knowledge and OCCAM projects).

If you have questions, comments and suggestions on how to improve artifact submission, reviewing, customization and reuse, please do not hesitate to get in touch with the AE steering committee!
