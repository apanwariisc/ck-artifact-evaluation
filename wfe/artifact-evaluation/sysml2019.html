Artifact Evaluation for SysML 2019

[ Back to SysML 2019 conference website ]

Authors of accepted SysML'19 papers are invited to formally submit supporting material (code, data, workflows, results) to the Artifact Evaluation process (AE). AE is run by a separate committee whose task is to assess how submitted artifacts support the work described in accepted papers while reproducing at least some experiments. This submission is voluntary and will not influence the final decision regarding the papers.

All papers that successfully go through AE will receive a set of official ACM badges of approval printed on the papers themselves and available as meta information in the ACM Digital Library (it is now possible to search for papers with specific badges in ACM DL). Authors of such papers will have an option to include Artifact Appendix to the camera-ready paper (up to 2 pages).

You need to prepare your artifacts and appendix using the following guidelines and submit them via the SysML AE website (TBA) before January 18,2019. Your submission will be then reviewed according to the following guidelines. Please, do not forget to provide a list of hardware, software, benchmark and data set dependencies in your artifact abstract - this is essential to find appropriate evaluators!

If you have questions, please check AE FAQs and get in touch with AE chairs!
