Motivation

Reproducing experimental results from computer systems papers and building upon them is becoming extremely challenging and time consuming. Major issues include ever changing and possibly proprietary software and hardware, lack of common tools and interfaces, stochastic behavior of computer systems, lack of common experimental methodology, and lack of universally accepted mechanisms for knowledge exchange [ 1, 2 ].

That is why we are helping to organize reproducibility initiatives and so-called Artifact Evaluations* to validate results from published papers by independent reviewers - please check out "submission" and "reviewing" guidelines for further details. Papers that successfully pass such validation process receive a set of ACM reproducibility badges printed on the papers themselves. At the same time, we are working with the community to enable reusable research components and reproducible workflows (pipelines) shared along with papers - this is needed to automate very tedious and ad-hoc validation and thus reduce the burden placed on both authors and reviewers: see cKnowledge.org for more details!

Our eventual goal is to develop a common methodology and tools for truly open, collaborative, reproducible, reusable and trusable research - your feedback is essential to make it happen! If you have any questions, comments or suggestions, do not hesitate to get in touch or participate in public discussions via LinkedIn group, CK public group and AE public group.


* We would like to thank the ACM taskforce on reproducibility, Prof. Shriram Krishnamurthi (see original artifact evaluation at programming language conferences including ACM SIGPLAN PLDI), Matthias Hauswirth (original AE badge, related paper), and all our colleagues for very fruitful discussions and feedback!
