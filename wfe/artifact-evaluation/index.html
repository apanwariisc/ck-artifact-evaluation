Our motivation

Reproducing experimental results from computer systems papers and building upon them is extremely challenging and time consuming. Major issues include continuously changing and possibly proprietary software and hardware, lack of common tools and interfaces, stochastic behavior of computer systems, lack of common experimental methodology, and lack of universally accepted mechanisms for knowledge exchange.

After personally suffering from the same problems during our research on machine learning and computer systems [ 1, 2, 3 ], we decided to help systems and ML conferences to organize reproducibility initiatives since 2014. We have been helping to validate results from published papers by the independent reviewers while collaborating with ACM on a common methodology and tools to automate artifact evaluation - please check our "submission" and "reviewing" guidelines and unified Artifact Appendices for further details.


Papers that successfully pass such artifact evaluation process receive a set of ACM reproducibility badges printed on the papers themselves.

At the same time, we are working with the community to enable portable workflows, reusable research components and "live" papers (where results are continuously validated by the community). Feel free to test the live demo of our open-source portable workflows supporting our eventual goal to develop a common methodology and open-source tools for collaborative, reproducible, reusable and sustainable research!

If you have questions or suggestions, do not hesitate to participate in our public discussions via our LinkedIn group, CK mailing list, the Artifact Evaluation google group, and the Slack #artifact-evaluation channel.
