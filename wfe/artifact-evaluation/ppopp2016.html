Artifact Evaluation for PPoPP 2016

[ Back to PPoPP 2016 conference website ]

Authors of accepted PPoPP 2016 papers will be invited to formally submit their supporting materials to the Artifact Evaluation process. The Artifact Evaluation process is run by a separate committee whose task is to assess how the artifacts support the work described in the papers. This submission is voluntary and will not influence the final decision regarding the papers. Papers that successfully go through the Artifact Evaluation process will receive a seal of approval printed on the papers themselves. Authors of accepted papers are then encouraged to make these materials publicly available upon publication of the proceedings, by including them as "source materials" in the Digital Library.

Artifact packaging guidelines

Please, check out our packaging and submission guidelines to prepare your artifacts for submission.

How to submit

Please, upload your submission at the joint PPoPP/CGO EasyChair website. Submit your paper title (do not forget to add PPoPP 2016), author list, original abstract, and a pdf of your paper with an appendix describing how to download and validate your artifacts.

Reviewing process

Please, check out our reviewing guidelines to know how your artifacts will be evaluated.

Feedback

We consider Artifact Evaluation as a continuous learning curve - our eventual goal is to collaboratively develop common experimental and artifact sharing methodology for computer system's research. After running AE at PPoPP 2015 for the first time, we received an overall positive feedback from the community and, therefore, continue this experiment. However, we also experienced various issues which you may check out in our online presentation. Based on this experience and in collaboration with ACM, we are preparing revised submission and reviewing guidelines for PPoPP 2016. If you have questions, comments and suggestions on how to improve packaging, submission and reviewing process, please do not hesitate to get in touch!
